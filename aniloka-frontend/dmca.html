Copyright Takedown Policy (DMCA)

Last updated: July 2026

AniLoka responds to clear notices of alleged copyright infringement in line with standard notice-and-takedown practice. This page explains how to submit one.

1. Filing a Takedown Notice

Send a written notice to our designated agent (contact below) that includes all of the following:

  1. A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed.
  2. The exact URL(s) or location on AniLoka where the allegedly infringing material appears.
  3. Your contact information — name, address, phone number, and email.
  4. A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  5. A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on their behalf.
  6. Your physical or electronic signature.

2. Designated Agent

Send takedown notices to: [dmca@yourdomain.com]

3. What Happens Next

Valid notices are reviewed promptly. If the notice is valid, the content is removed or disabled, and the uploader is notified.

4. Counter-Notice

If you believe your content was removed in error, you may submit a counter-notice with: your contact information, identification of the removed material, a statement under penalty of perjury that you believe the removal was a mistake, and your consent to the jurisdiction of the relevant courts. We will forward valid counter-notices to the original complainant.

5. Repeat Infringers

Accounts that are the subject of repeated valid takedown notices will be permanently terminated.
